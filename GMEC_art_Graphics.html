Art & Fashion

The art issue of Mode Lifestyle Magazine with Madonna on the cover, encapsulates the fashion & music icons own perspective of the new age of fashion style. This is an interesting article about Madonna's new take on her life in music and what her music offers the new generation of fans coming forth as we head into the future of the 21st century. - Gwenevere Carter

In this issue we explore art in the form of music by taking a look at Madonna who is experiencing a form of rebirth as expressed in her album Madame X. We then take a look at the works of celebrated international fashion photographer Iran Issa-khan after an intimate interview at her Miami residence. The paintings of Lúcio Carvalho come next and are inspired by scenography as well as the Baroque, Rococo and Renaissance era. Next stop is to France and a look at the one-of-a-kind jewelry pieces handcrafted by jewelry artist Vanévy Okarane. We look forward to featuring some of Vanévy Okarane’s beautiful jewelry in future editorials in 2020. When most people think of art, furniture is not the first thing that comes to mind, unless of course you are really into furniture and the history of furniture design. “Warm Tones” – Mezzo Collection shows the art in furniture design and brings the 50s and 60s era design squarely into the modern. As always we are happy to receive communications from our family of readers and look forward to hearing from you by email, phone, or even perhaps in person at an event somewhere soon. ~ Alexander Michaels

Watercolor Painting

Watercolor (American English), or watercolour (Commonwealth English; see spelling differences), also aquarelle (French: [akwaʁɛl]; from Italian diminutive of Latin aqua 'water'), is a painting method in which the paints are made of pigments suspended in a water-based solution. Watercolor refers to both the medium and the resulting artwork.

Aquarelles painted with water-soluble colored ink instead of modern water colors are called aquarellum atramento (Latin for "aquarelle made with ink") by experts. However, this term has now tended to pass out of use. Watercolor paint is an ancient form of painting, if not the most ancient form of art itself. In East Asia, watercolor painting with inks is referred to as brush painting or scroll painting. In Chinese, Korean and Japanese painting it has been the dominant medium, often in monochrome black or browns, often using inkstick or other pigments. India, Ethiopia and other countries have long watercolor painting traditions as well.
